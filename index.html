This site is expected to be a personal knowledge management.

Five Stages in Drefus Model

We’ll talk about model, rule or something, but be aware of tool trap, and always think about context.

  • Novices, have little or no previous experience in this skill area. They are very concerned about their ability to succeed; with little exp, they really don’t know whether their actions will all turn out OK. They just want to accomplish an immediate goal then learning it. They need recipe such as ‘if x happens, do y!’

  • Advanced Beginners, they can try tasks on their own, start using advice in the corret context, but they have no holistic understanding and really don’t want it yet. For instance, when present charts and figures showing sales projections, less exp staff will tend to dismiss it as not being relevant to their individual job. Obviously, they are wrong.

  • Competent, as this stage, they can now develop conceptual models of the problem domain and work with those models effectively. They can troubleshoot problems in their own and begin to figure out how to solve novel problems–ones they haven’t faced before. They can begin to seek out and apply advice from experts and use it effectively. But their ability isn’t enough to support them for relection and self-correction.

  • Proficient, they need the ‘big picture’. They will seek out and want to understand the larger conceptual framework around this skill. They can correct previous poor task performance. They can reflect on how they’ve done and revise their approach to perform better the next time. They can learn from the exp of others, read case studies, listen to water-cooler gossip of failed projects, see what others have done, and learn effectively from the story, even though you didn’t participate in it first hand.

  • Expert, magic level…

Personal Knowledge Management

A piece of knowledge may be software systems, algorithms, data structures, philosophical ideas, etc. Based on the drefus model, I intend to divide the learning process of a knowledge into several stages.

  • Getting Started. Simply understand what it is and how to use it out of the box. For example, use kubeadm to build a local k8s cluster, run simple resource creation or view commands on it
  • Advanced. Understand its internal principles and be able to choose the right solution in different scenarios. For example, understand how network plug-ins are used in k8s clusters and what modes are available.
  • In-depth. Understanding of implementation details, able to locate and solve problems. For example, solve the problem that a certain request causes the abnormality of the k8s component during the stress test.
  • Big Picture. Provide complete and reliable solutions, and control the landing process.
